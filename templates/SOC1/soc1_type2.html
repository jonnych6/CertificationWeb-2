SOC 1 Type 2

How to reach SOC 1 Type 1 compliance

For many organizations, achieving SOC 1 Type 2 compliance is a critical milestone toward ensuring the reliability and integrity of their financial reporting over a specified period. To comply with SOC 1 Type 2, you need to establish and document robust internal controls over financial reporting, demonstrating that these controls are not only suitably designed but also operating effectively over time. We've developed a detailed checklist to guide you through your SOC 1 Type 2 implementation and compliance process. This checklist includes the following steps:

Step Description Additional Steps & Details
1 Scope Definition Define the scope similarly to a Type 1 audit, including 24/7 mechanical monitoring.
2 Organization of Project Team

Assign a project manager to oversee the SOC 1 Type 2 audit.

Constitute a team with IT, finance, compliance, and operations on board and ensure there is continuous monitoring and assessment capabilities.

3 Documentation and Risk Assessment

Update documentation and conduct risk assessments throughout the audit period.

Grant a risk assessment, noting possible jeopardy to financial reporting controls.

4 Develop and Implement Controls Design and build controls addressing identified risks.
5 Continuous Evidence Collection ollect and organize evidence of control efficacy throughout the audit period.
6 Regular Internal Review Perform periodic internal assessments to test and adjust controls as needed.
7 Ongoing Communication with the Auditor Maintain consistent communication with the auditor during interim testing.
8 Resolving Interim Findings Address issues identified during interim testing to ensure they are resolved before the end of the audit period.
9 Completion of Audit and Report

Collaborate with the auditor to finalize the review of controls for the audit period.

Review and issue the final Type 2 report, ensuring it accurately represents the operational effectiveness of the controls.

10 Post-Audit Activities

Implement modifications suggested in the audit to improve control effectiveness.

Prepare for long-term compliance and the next audit.
