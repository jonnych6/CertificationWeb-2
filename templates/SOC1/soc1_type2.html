SOC 1 Type 2

How to reach SOC 1 Type 1 compliance

For many organizations, achieving SOC 1 Type 2 compliance is a critical milestone toward ensuring the reliability and integrity of their financial reporting over a specified period. To comply with SOC 1 Type 2, you need to establish and document robust internal controls over financial reporting, demonstrating that these controls are not only suitably designed but also operating effectively over time. We've developed a detailed checklist to guide you through your SOC 1 Type 2 implementation and compliance process. This checklist includes the following steps:

Step # Description Additional Steps & Details
1 Scope Definition

Identify and document services and controls relevant to financial reporting.

Identify classes of transaction, types of systems involved, and the applications applicable.

2 Organization of Project Team

Assign a project manager to oversee the SOC 1 Type 1 audit.

Constitute a team with IT, finance, compliance, and operations on board.

3 Documentation and Risk Assessment

Document all relevant processes, systems, and controls.

Grant a risk assessment, noting possible jeopardy to financial reporting controls.

4 Develop and Implement Controls

Design and build controls addressing identified risks.

Review to verify prevention of misstatements in the financial statements.

5 Document Preparation

Gather supporting documentation.

Prepare good and accessible documentation for the auditor.
6 Look for an Auditor

Select a reputable Auditing firm or CPA for SOC 1 reports.

Form a mutual understanding of the scope and period of operations.

7 The Period of Engagement

Ensure that the auditor understands the control environment.

Allow access to relevant documents and personnel.
8 Results Check and Adjust

Review the preliminary results with auditor.

All the discrepancies, misunderstandings, errors must be resolved before final report.

9 Sharing Final Report

Review the Type 1 report for accuracy and completeness.

Distribute the final report to affected stakeholders who rely on the proper implementation of controls in financial reporting. Ensure stakeholders understand the report and its implications.
