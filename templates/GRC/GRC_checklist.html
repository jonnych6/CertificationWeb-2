GRC

How to achieve GRC compliance

For organizations seeking to achieve comprehensive GRC compliance, this checklist provides a detailed guide through the GRC implementation and compliance process. It includes steps to help ensure that your organization effectively manages governance, risk, and compliance requirements.

Step Description Additional Steps & Details
1 Define Scope and Objectives - Identify the scope of the GRC framework implementation.
- Set clear objectives for governance, risk management, and compliance.
- Align GRC objectives with the overall strategic goals of the organization.
2 Assemble GRC Team - Designate a project manager to oversee the GRC implementation.
- Build a cross-functional team including representatives from IT, security, compliance, risk management, and operations.
- Ensure team members are clear on their roles and responsibilities and maintain regular communication.
3 Conduct Risk Assessment - Identify potential risks that could impact the organization.
- Assess the likelihood and impact of identified risks.
- Prioritize risks based on their potential impact on the organization.
- Develop a risk mitigation plan to address high-priority risks.
4 Develop Governance Framework - Establish governance policies and procedures.
- Define roles and responsibilities for governance.
- Implement accountability frameworks to ensure compliance with governance policies.
- Regularly review and update governance policies and procedures.
5 Implement Risk Management Strategies - Develop risk management policies and procedures.
- Implement risk mitigation controls to reduce the impact of identified risks.
- Establish continuous monitoring and reporting mechanisms for risk management.
- Regularly review and update risk management strategies.
6 Ensure Regulatory Compliance - Identify relevant laws, regulations, and standards applicable to the organization.
- Develop compliance policies and procedures.
- Implement controls to ensure adherence to compliance requirements.
- Conduct regular compliance audits and assessments.
- Address any compliance gaps identified during audits.
7 Employee Training and Awareness - Develop a comprehensive training program focused on GRC principles and practices.
- Organize regular training sessions, workshops, and seminars.
- Update training materials and sessions with changes in policies and procedures.
- Include practical examples and case studies to enhance understanding.
- Conduct periodic assessments and quizzes to gauge employees' understanding and retention of the training material.
- Collect feedback to continuously improve the training program.
8 Gather and Maintain Evidence - Continuously collect evidence demonstrating the effectiveness of implemented controls.
- Types of evidence may include system logs, access control records, incident reports, change management records, and periodic audit reports.
- Store evidence in a centralized and secure repository accessible to authorized personnel.
- Prepare a summary report or dashboard highlighting key control activities and findings.
9 Conduct Internal Audits - Schedule regular internal audits to assess the effectiveness of governance, risk management, and compliance controls.
- Develop a comprehensive internal audit plan outlining the scope, objectives, and schedule.
- Document all findings, including deficiencies, weaknesses, or areas for improvement.
- Analyze root causes and develop remediation plans.
- Implement corrective actions and verify that changes effectively mitigate risks.
10 Select and Interact with External Auditors - Select external auditors experienced in GRC assessments.
- Start discussions and agree on timelines covering the required review period.
- Ensure clear understanding and alignment on the audit scope, objectives, and deliverables.
- Maintain regular communication with the auditor throughout the audit process.
- Provide the auditor with necessary documentation, access, and support.
11 Review and Act on Audit Findings - Discuss interim feedback or findings from the auditor and take necessary actions.
- Develop and implement action plans to address any tracked issues.
- Verify the effectiveness of remediation efforts.
- Assist the auditor in completing the final assessment and provide additional information promptly.
- Review the final audit report thoroughly.
- Distribute the final report to appropriate stakeholders and discuss responses and required actions.
12 Ongoing GRC Program - Implement decisions based on the auditor's suggestions.
- Document and verify all changes made in response to the auditorâ€™s suggestions.
- Adopt continuous monitoring and periodic control review processes.
- Conduct regular risk assessments and update controls based on new threats.
- Maintain thorough documentation of all compliance activities.
- Continue investing in employee training and awareness programs.
- Foster a culture of integrity, accountability, and continuous improvement across the organization.
