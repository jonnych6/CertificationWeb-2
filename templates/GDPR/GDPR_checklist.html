GDPR

How to achieve GDPR compliance

For many organizations, achieving GDPR compliance is crucial for safeguarding personal data and expanding their operations within the European Union. To comply with GDPR, you need to establish a robust and strategic data protection framework. We've created a comprehensive checklist to guide your GDPR implementation and compliance. This checklist includes the following steps:

Step Description Additional Steps & Details
1 Awareness and Training Awareness: Ensure decision-makers and key personnel are aware of GDPR requirements.
Training: Provide GDPR-specific training to all employees and keep records.
2 Information Audit Data Inventory: Document personal data, its source, and data sharing details.
Data Mapping: Create data flow diagrams to visualize data movement.
3 Lawful Basis for Processing Identify Lawful Basis: Determine and document the lawful basis for each processing activity.
Communicate Basis: Include lawful basis in privacy notices.
4 Data Subjects’ Rights Procedures: Implement and ensure procedures for handling data subjects' rights requests meet GDPR timescales.
5 Consent Review Consent Mechanisms: Ensure consent is freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous.
Record Keeping: Maintain records of consent and provide withdrawal mechanisms.
6 Children’s Data Age Verification: Implement systems to verify age and obtain parental consent for children under 16.
7 Data Breaches Detection and Response: Implement procedures to detect, report, and investigate breaches.
Notification: Notify authorities within 72 hours and affected individuals if necessary.
8 Data Protection by Design and Default Integrate Data Protection: Embed data protection into business practices.
DPIAs: Data Protection Impact Assessments for high-risk activities.
9 Data Protection Officers (DPO) Designation: Appoint a DPO if required and ensure they have resources, independence, and authority.
10 Internal Policies and Procedures Review Policies: Regularly review and update internal data protection policies and procedures to ensure ongoing compliance.
Enforce Procedures: Ensure all policies are enforced and integrated into daily operations.
11 Third-party Contracts Review Contracts: Ensure third-party contracts include GDPR-compliant terms.
Due Diligence: Ensure third parties implement appropriate security measures.
12 Privacy Notices Review and Update: Ensure privacy notices provide clear information on data processing activities.
Transparency: Include purposes, lawful bases, retention periods, rights, and data transfers.
13 Security Measures Technical Measures: Implement encryption, access controls, and data anonymization.
Organizational Measures: Enforce policies, employee training, and regular audits.
14 Data Minimization and Retention Data Minimization: Collect and process only necessary data.
Retention Policies: Establish, enforce, and regularly review data retention policies.
15 Record-Keeping and Documentation Records of Processing Activities: Maintain detailed records and ensure documentation is up-to-date and available for inspection.
16 Raise Awareness Holistic Approach: Involve all employees in GDPR compliance, raise awareness, and ensure third-party compliance.
17 Keep a Record of Data Processing Flows Data Flow Records: Track and regularly update records of data flows within your company.
Notify Corrections: Inform other companies if incorrect personal data is shared.
18 Review Current Privacy Notices Content Update: Update privacy policies to include data collection methods, lawful basis, usage, retention, and users' rights.
Cookie Policy: Maintain a current cookie policy using automated tools.
19 Check Your Rights for Individuals Review Procedures: Ensure procedures address individuals’ rights such as access, correction, portability, and deletion.
Practical Steps: Create response letters, update policies, and establish technical procedures.
20 Detect, Report, and Investigate Data Breaches Breach Procedures: Implement procedures to handle data breaches and notify authorities within 72 hours.
High-Risk Notifications: Inform affected individuals if necessary.
