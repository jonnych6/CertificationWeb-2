Everything you need to know about System and Organization Controls 2

What is SOC2 reporting?

SOC 2 (System and Organization Controls 2) reports are a set of criteria developed by the American Institute of CPAs (AICPA) to measure how well a service organization conducts and regulates its information. The criteria for managing customer data are based on five “trust service principles”—security, availability, processing integrity, confidentiality, and privacy.

Why do companies need SOC 2?

SOC 2 compliance is critical for SaaS companies and other technology service providers that store customer data in the cloud. It is a way to ensure that service providers manage data securely to protect the interests of their organization and the privacy of their clients.

What does SOC 2 do?

SOC 2 reports are unique to each organization, unlike PCI DSS, which has very rigid requirements. Each company designs its own controls to comply with one or more of the trust service principles. These reports provide a detailed analysis of controls relevant to security, availability, processing integrity, confidentiality, and privacy.

What are the SOC 2 compliance levels?

  • SOC 2 Type I Report: Audit of the design of controls and report on the suitability of the design of controls at a specific point in time.
  • SOC 2 Type II Report: Audit of the operational effectiveness of controls and report on the suitability of the design and operating effectiveness of controls over a period of time.

In summary:

A SOC 2 Type 1 report evaluates the design of controls at a specific point in time, while a SOC 2 Type 2 report assesses both the design and operating effectiveness of controls over a period, typically 12 months. Type II reports provide greater assurance than Type I, which are often used initially to identify and prioritize relevant controls for future evaluations.

Who needs a SOC 2 report?

Service organizations that handle customer data, especially those providing SaaS, cloud computing, and IT managed services, need to submit a SOC 2 report. This includes:

  1. SaaS Providers: Companies offering software-as-a-service solutions.
  2. Data Centers and IT Service Providers: Organizations that host or manage critical financial applications or data for their clients need to show that their systems are secure and that their controls are effective.
  3. Cloud Computing Providers: Companies offering cloud infrastructure or platform services.
  4. Managed IT Services: Providers of managed IT services.
  5. Healthcare Services: Organizations handling sensitive patient data.
  6. Financial Services: Firms managing financial data.

How much does it cost to conduct a SOC 2 audit?

The cost of a SOC 2 audit can vary widely depending on several factors:

  1. Size of your organization
  2. The complexity of your internal organisation and practices
  3. Scope of your SOC 1 Compliance
  4. Where your organization located
  5. Audit period

When you hire an auditor, they will be able to give you an estimated cost for your audit.

Are SOC 2 reports mandatory?

SOC 2 reports are not legally required, but they are often requested by clients or partners as part of vendor risk management programs. Having a SOC 2 report demonstrates that your organization is committed to maintaining high standards of data security and privacy.

How long is a SOC 2 report valid?

SOC 2 reports are typically valid for 12 months from the date of issuance. Organizations must undergo regular audits to maintain compliance and provide up-to-date assurances to clients and stakeholders.
