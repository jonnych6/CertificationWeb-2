Everything you need to know about System and Organization Controls 1

What is SOC1 reporting?

SOC is a framework developed by the American Institute of Certified Public Accountants (AICPA) to demonstrate that an organization adheres to best practices in business management, such as making sound financial and information security decisions. There are currently three types of SOC reports that provide different perspectives on how your business operates: SOC 1, SOC 2, and SOC 3.

These reports allow clients to assess the potential risk of working with you, which can influence their decision to hire you as a vendor. A SOC 1 report, in particular, can verify the controls in place to reduce inaccurate financial reporting, which could have an impact on your customers' financials. It includes practices such as personnel policies, data manipulation prevention measures, and more.

The audit report will examine service organization's internal controls over financial reporting (ICFR). These reports are intended to reassure users and auditors that the service organization's controls are appropriately designed and operating effectively to mitigate risks that may affect their clients' financial statements.

Two types of SOC1 reports

  • SOC 1 Type I Report:Describes the service organization's system and evaluates the suitability of the control design at a specific point in time. It provides an overview of the controls in place and assesses whether they are properly designed to achieve their goals.
  • SOC 1 Type II Report: Contains the same information as a Type I report, but it also provides a detailed assessment of the operational effectiveness of the controls over a given time period. It assesses whether the controls were consistently applied and operated as intended throughout the review period.

In summary:

A Type I SOC 1 report evaluates the design of controls at a specific point in time, while a Type II SOC 1 report assesses both the design and operating effectiveness of controls over a period, typically 12 months. Type II reports provide greater assurance than Type I, which are often used initially to identify and prioritize relevant controls for future evaluations.

Who needs a SOC 1 report?

Service organizations that have an impact on their clients' financial reporting are required to submit a SOC 1 report. Usually, these groups consist of:

  1. Payroll Processors: Companies that manage payroll services for other businesses need to demonstrate the accuracy and reliability of their financial controls.
  2. Data Centers and IT Service Providers:Organizations that host or manage critical financial applications or data for their clients need to show that their systems are secure and that their controls are effective.
  3. Accounting and Financial Services Firms:Firms that provide outsourced accounting, bookkeeping, or financial services must ensure their clients that their internal controls over financial reporting are robust and reliable.
  4. Benefit Plan Administrators:Companies that administer employee benefit plans need to verify that they have proper controls in place to accurately manage and report on these plans.
  5. Insurance Claims Processors:Organizations that handle insurance claims processing need to demonstrate that their controls can accurately and securely manage financial transactions.
  6. Investment and Asset Management Firms: These firms must ensure that their controls over financial reporting are sound to protect their clients' investments and assets.

How much does it cost to conduct a SOC 1 audit?

A SOC 1 audit typically costs between $10,000 and $50,000, or more. The cost of your audit will be affected by several factors, including:

  1. Size of your organization
  2. The complexity of your internal organisation and practices
  3. Scope of your SOC 1 Compliance
  4. Where is your organization located?

When you hire an auditor, they will be able to give you an estimated cost for your audit.

Are SOC 1 reports mandatory?

SOC 1 is not a legal requirement for any organization, and you will not face fines or penalties if you do not have one. However, some clients will only work with vendors for specific services if they have a SOC 1 report. If your organization has an impact on your customers' financial reporting, you may be impeding your company's growth by failing to obtain a SOC 1.

How long is a SOC 1 report valid?

Once the audit is completed, your auditor will issue your SOC 1 report. This report will remain valid for one year. To maintain SOC 1 compliance, you will need to undergo another audit; it is best to begin the process of obtaining your new report before your current one expires. These subsequent audits are typically easier than the initial audit because your SOC 1 controls are already in place and your team is familiar with the audit process.
