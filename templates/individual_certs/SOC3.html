Everything you need to know about System and Organization Controls 3

What is SOC3?

SOC 3 (System and Organization Controls 3) reports are a set of criteria developed by the American Institute of CPAs (AICPA) to measure how well a service organization conducts and regulates its information. Unlike SOC 2, SOC 3 reports are designed for a general audience and are less detailed, making them suitable for public distribution. The criteria for managing customer data are based on five “trust service principles”—security, availability, processing integrity, confidentiality, and privacy.

Why do companies need SOC 3?

SOC 3 compliance is essential for service organizations that want to provide transparency to their customers and stakeholders about how they manage and protect data. SOC 3 reports demonstrate that a service provider adheres to high standards for data security and privacy, which can enhance trust and credibility.

What does SOC 3 do?

SOC 3 reports provide a high-level overview of the controls implemented by a service organization to meet the trust service principles. These reports are intended for a broad audience, including customers, partners, and the general public, and do not include the detailed testing and results found in SOC 2 reports.

What are the SOC 3 compliance criteria?

# Principle Description
1 Security Information and systems are protected against unauthorized access (both physical and logical).
2 Availability Information and systems are available for operation and use as committed or agreed.
3 Processing Integrity System processing is complete, valid, accurate, timely, and authorized.
4 Confidentiality Information designated as confidential is protected as committed or agreed.
5 Privacy Personal information is collected, used, retained, disclosed, and disposed of in conformity with the commitments in the entity’s privacy notice.

In summary:

SOC 3 reports provide a summary of a service organization’s controls relevant to security, availability, processing integrity, confidentiality, and privacy. These reports are less detailed than SOC 2 reports and are intended for a broad audience to demonstrate the organization’s commitment to high standards of data protection.

Who needs a SOC 3 report?

Service organizations that want to provide a high-level assurance about their data protection practices to a broad audience should consider obtaining a SOC 3 report. This includes:

  1. SaaS Providers: Companies offering software-as-a-service solutions.
  2. Data Centers and IT Service Providers: Organizations that host or manage critical financial applications or data for their clients need to show that their systems are secure and that their controls are effective.
  3. Cloud Computing Providers: Companies offering cloud infrastructure or platform services.
  4. Managed IT Services: Providers of managed IT services.
  5. Healthcare Services: Organizations handling sensitive patient data.
  6. Financial Services: Firms managing financial data.

How much does it cost to conduct a SOC 3 audit?

The cost of a SOC 3 audit can vary depending on several factors:

  1. Size of the company
  2. Complexity of the IT environment
  3. Risk profile
  4. Number of locations
  5. Audit period

When you hire an auditor, they will be able to give you an estimated cost for your audit.

Are SOC 3 reports mandatory?

SOC 3 reports are not legally required, but they are beneficial for demonstrating an organization’s commitment to data security and privacy. Many clients and partners may request a SOC 3 report as part of their vendor risk management process.

How long is a SOC 3 report valid?

SOC 3 reports are typically valid for 12 months from the date of issuance. Organizations must undergo regular audits to maintain compliance and provide up-to-date assurances to clients and stakeholders.
