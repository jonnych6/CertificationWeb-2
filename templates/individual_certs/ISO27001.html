Everything you need to know about International Organization of Standardization (ISO)'s 27001 certification

What is ISO?

ISO stands for the International Organization of Standardization, although it is not a direct acronym for that name. Because ISO is a multinational organization headquartered in Switzerland that operates in multiple languages, the organization developed its own abbreviation to avoid favoring one over another.

ISO 27001 is sometimes referred to as ISO/IEC 27001:2022. The International Organization of Standardization and the International Electrotechnical Commission collaborated to develop this standard, which is known as ISO/IEC. The current version of the standard was published in 2022.

ISO 27001 is widely regarded as the global gold standard for information security and supporting assets. Obtaining ISO 27001 certification can help an organization demonstrate its security practices to potential customers all over the world.

What is ISO?

The International Organization for Standardization (ISO) and the International Electrotechnical Commission (IEC) collaborated to develop the ISO 27001 standard, which enables businesses to design secure systems and demonstrate their security posture through a certification process. ISO 27001 focuses on a company's information security management system (ISMS), which is a set of policies and procedures that describe how an organization's processes contribute to data security.

To obtain ISO 27001 certification, you must go through an audit that confirms you have met the compliance requirements and addressed any potential system risks. The standard consists of ten clauses and 114 controls divided into fourteen categories that represent potential security measures that an organization may consider based on its needs and risks. While you must comply with all ten clauses, you do not need to implement all 114 ISO 27001 controls to become certified.

Who is ISO 27001 for? And why is it important?

The purpose of ISO 27001 certification is to demonstrate to your customers and prospects that security is a top priority for your company. While ISO 27001 is not a legal requirement, your customers may require certification before doing business with you. ISO 27001 is applicable to organizations that handle or manage customer data, and it is particularly common among SaaS providers, data storage solutions, data processing and analytics tools, and other data-service platforms.

When deciding whether or not to obtain ISO 27001 certification, you should also consider the location of your customers. SOC 2 is a compliance standard similar to ISO 27001 that is primarily used in North America but is gaining traction globally, whereas ISO 27001 is an international standard used all over the world. If you work with clients outside of North America, ISO 27001 is likely to be important to them.

Many businesses decide they need both a SOC 2 and an ISO 27001 certification if they have customers in North America and other countries around the world. Given that many of the controls overlap, it is common for organizations seeking compliance in both standards to undergo audits concurrently.

A completed certificate can show prospects, customers, partners, and other stakeholders that your company has a strong information security posture. While ISO 27001 certification is not legally required, many large organizations insist on it for their vendors. This is done to ensure that the vendors with whom they do business protect their data properly.

Which industries and businesses benefit the most from ISO 27001?

Any organization with a significant digital footprint or those involved in processing and storing sensitive information will benefit from ISO 27001, as it helps establish a comprehensive framework for managing information security risks and demonstrates a commitment to protecting data..
