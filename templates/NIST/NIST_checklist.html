NIST

How to reach NIST compliance

For many organizations, achieving NIST compliance is a crucial step toward strengthening their security posture and ensuring robust protection of sensitive information. To comply with NIST guidelines, you must implement a comprehensive security framework tailored to safeguard your data and systems. We've created a detailed checklist to help you navigate the NIST implementation and certification process. This checklist includes the following essential steps:

NIST 800-171 Compliance Checklist

Step Description Additional Steps & Details
1 Assess the Scope of the Contract - Determine Applicability: Find out if your organization needs to follow the NIST 800-171 standard.
- Understand Contract Scope: Assess the contract’s scope to determine which NIST 800-171 requirements apply.
- Adherence to the Defense Federal Acquisition Regulation Supplement (DFARS).
- Compliance with Cybersecurity Maturity Model Certification (CMMC) requirements.
- Obtaining and maintaining necessary security clearances for personnel.
- Ensuring secure handling and storage of classified information.
- Meeting specified technical and performance standards.
- Delivering products or services within the agreed timeline and budget.
2 Identify Whether You’re Dealing with CUI - Determine CUI Presence: Identify if you’re dealing with Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI).
- Conduct an Audit: Perform a complete audit of your company’s systems and data flows, including third-party contractors.
- Recognize Keywords: Look for terms like “critical infrastructure,” “export control,” or “statutory” that indicate potential CUI.
3 Classify the Data - Categorize CUI: Once identified, categorize CUI according to its type. Different types of CUI require varying levels of protection.
- Use NIST Categories: Follow NIST 800-171’s 20 categories of CUI, which include:
  • Critical Infrastructure
  • Defense
  • Export Control
  • Intelligence
  • Procurement and Acquisition
  • Financial
  • Law Enforcement
  • Legal
  • International Agreements
  • Natural and Cultural Resources
  • Nuclear
  • Provisional
  • Patents
  • NATO
  • Privacy
  • Proprietary Business Information
  • Statistical
  • Tax
  • Transportation
4 Gather Appropriate Documentation - System and Network Architecture: Create detailed diagrams and descriptions of your system and network setup.
- System Boundaries: Clearly define where your systems begin and end, including interfaces with other systems.
- Data Flow: Document how data moves through your systems, including storage and processing points.
- Personnel Records: Maintain records of staff roles and responsibilities, including access to CUI and training records.
- Processes and Procedures: Write down procedures and policies for handling CUI, security measures, and incident response plans.
- Anticipated Changes: Document any planned changes to your systems or processes that could affect compliance.
5 Conduct a Gap Analysis - Pre-Assessment: Conduct a pre-assessment to determine your readiness for certification.
- Identify Gaps: Perform a gap analysis to identify existing gaps, starting with primary access control requirements.
- Document Findings: Record any flaws or control gaps to address them.
6 Develop and Test Baseline Controls - Identify Controls: Select security and privacy controls that comply with legal requirements, security standards, and policies.
- Ensure Coverage: Verify that baseline controls cover all 14 control families listed in NIST 800-171.
- Test Controls: Regularly test the selected controls to ensure they are effective.
7 Gather the Right Evidence - Collect Evidence: Gather the necessary evidence for your NIST audit, focusing on the 14 NIST 800-171 criteria.
- Document Preservation: Ensure that all evidence, including compromised systems, is preserved and documented properly for potential legal proceedings.
8 Conduct Continuous Monitoring - Implement Monitoring: Establish continuous monitoring processes and technology to detect compliance and risk issues.
- Regular Testing: Regularly test controls and improve them when vulnerabilities are identified.
- Update Controls: Adjust controls as necessary to address any detected issues or changes in the operational environment.

NIST CSF

Step Description Additional Steps & Details
1 Identify - Asset Management - Inventory all physical devices and systems.
- Inventory all software platforms and applications.
- Map organizational communication and data flows.
- Identify and document external information systems.
- Define roles and responsibilities for cybersecurity.
2 Identify - Business Environment - Understand and document the organization’s mission, objectives, and activities.
- Identify organizational dependencies and critical functions.
3 Identify - Governance - Establish and document cybersecurity policies.
- Define roles, responsibilities, and authority for cybersecurity decisions.
4 Identify - Risk Assessment - Identify and assess cybersecurity risks to organizational operations.
- Conduct regular risk assessments and document findings.
- Review and prioritize risks based on their impact.
5 Identify - Risk Management Strategy - Define risk management policies and processes.
- Establish risk tolerance levels and thresholds.
6 Identify - Supply Chain Risk Management - Identify and assess risks from third-party suppliers and partners.
- Implement measures to mitigate supply chain risks.
- Document supply chain security policies.
7 Protect - Identity Management and Access Control - Implement access control measures.
- Enforce least privilege access principles.
- Use identity and access management (IAM) solutions.
8 Protect - Awareness and Training - Conduct regular security awareness training for all employees.
- Maintain records of training sessions and materials.
9 Protect - Data Security - Implement data protection measures, including encryption.
- Ensure secure handling and storage of sensitive data.
- Regularly review and update data protection policies.
10 Protect - Information Protection Processes and Procedures - Develop and enforce security policies and procedures.
- Conduct regular reviews and updates of security policies.
- Document and maintain incident response procedures.
11 Protect - Maintenance - Schedule and document regular maintenance activities for information systems.
- Ensure maintenance activities do not compromise security.
12 Protect - Protective Technology - Implement security technologies, such as firewalls, intrusion detection systems, and antivirus software.
- Regularly update and patch security technologies.
- Monitor the effectiveness of protective technologies.
13 Detect - Anomalies and Events - Develop and implement monitoring tools to detect anomalies.
- Establish processes to analyze and triage detected events.
- Document and respond to detected anomalies.
14 Detect - Security Continuous Monitoring - Implement continuous monitoring of networks and systems.
- Regularly review monitoring data and respond to alerts.
- Ensure monitoring tools are updated and functioning correctly.
15 Detect - Detection Processes - Develop and maintain detection processes.
- Test and update detection processes regularly.
- Document detection procedures and incident response steps.
16 Respond - Response Planning - Develop and maintain an incident response plan.
- Ensure the incident response plan is tested and updated regularly.
- Assign roles and responsibilities for incident response.
17 Respond - Communications - Establish communication protocols for incident response.
- Define roles and responsibilities for communication during incidents.
- Document communication procedures and ensure they are followed during incidents.
18 Respond - Analysis - Analyze detected incidents to understand their impact and scope.
- Document incident analysis and findings.
- Use findings to improve response strategies.
19 Respond - Mitigation - Implement measures to contain and mitigate the impact of incidents.
- Test mitigation measures to ensure effectiveness.
- Document mitigation procedures and outcomes.
20 Respond - Improvements - Conduct post-incident reviews to identify lessons learned.
- Update response plans and processes based on review findings.
- Implement improvements to prevent future incidents.
21 Recover - Recovery Planning - Develop and implement recovery plans for critical systems and data.
- Ensure recovery plans are tested and updated regularly.
- Document recovery procedures and responsibilities.
22 Recover - Communications - Communicate recovery activities and status to stakeholders.
- Ensure clear and timely communication during the recovery process.
- Document communication procedures and ensure they are followed during recovery.

Checklist for NIST 800-53

Step Description Additional Steps & Details
1 Access Control (AC) - Implement identity and access management (IAM) solutions.
- Enforce least privilege access principles.
- Implement multi-factor authentication.
- Monitor and review access control mechanisms regularly.
2 Awareness and Training (AT) - Provide regular security awareness training to all employees.
- Maintain records of training sessions.
- Update training materials based on current threats and best practices.
3 Audit and Accountability (AU) - Implement logging and monitoring solutions.
- Regularly review and analyze audit logs.
- Ensure logs are protected from unauthorized access.
4 Configuration Management (CM) - Establish baseline configurations for all systems.
- Implement change management processes.
- Conduct regular configuration audits.
5 Contingency Planning (CP) - Develop disaster recovery and business continuity plans.
- Conduct regular testing of contingency plans.
- Ensure data backup and restore procedures are in place.
6 Identification and Authentication (IA) - Use multi-factor authentication for access to systems.
- Implement identity verification processes.
- Manage identification credentials securely.
7 Incident Response (IR) - Develop an incident response plan.
- Establish an incident response team.
- Conduct regular incident response training and drills.
8 Maintenance (MA) - Schedule regular maintenance activities for information systems.
- Document all maintenance actions.
- Ensure maintenance activities do not compromise security.
9 Media Protection (MP) - Implement encryption for sensitive data stored on media.
- Establish procedures for secure media disposal.
- Control access to media containing sensitive information.
10 Physical and Environmental Protection (PE) - Implement physical access controls to secure facilities.
- Monitor and log physical access to sensitive areas.
- Maintain environmental controls to protect hardware.
11 Planning (PL) - Develop security plans for all information systems.
- Regularly review and update security plans.
- Ensure plans address all identified risks and threats.
12 Personnel Security (PS) - Conduct background checks for employees with access to sensitive information.
- Implement procedures for managing personnel termination.
- Enforce security policies for personnel behavior.
13 Risk Assessment (RA) - Conduct regular risk assessments to identify potential threats.
- Document and prioritize risk findings.
- Develop mitigation strategies for identified risks.
14 System and Services Acquisition (SA) - Define security requirements for new systems and services.
- Conduct security reviews of third-party services.
- Ensure procurement processes include security considerations.
15 System and Communications Protection (SC) - Implement network security controls such as firewalls and intrusion detection systems.
- Use encryption for data in transit and at rest.
- Regularly update and patch security controls.
16 System and Information Integrity (SI) - Implement patch management processes.
- Monitor systems for vulnerabilities and apply updates promptly.
- Use antivirus and anti-malware solutions.
