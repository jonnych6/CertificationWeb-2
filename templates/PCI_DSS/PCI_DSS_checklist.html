PCI DSS

How to reach PCI compliance

For many organizations, achieving PCI DSS compliance is a critical milestone for enhancing their security posture and gaining trust in the payments industry. To comply with PCI DSS, you must implement a robust security framework tailored to protect cardholder data. We've developed a detailed checklist to assist you in navigating the PCI DSS implementation and certification process. This checklist encompasses the following steps:

Step Description Additional Steps & Details
1 Scope and Identify: Identify All Cardholder Data Flows - Document all locations where cardholder data is stored, processed, or transmitted.
- Define scope of the Cardholder Data Environment (CDE), including all systems, networks, and physical locations.
2 Build and Maintain a Secure Network and Systems - Develop a network diagram illustrating all connections to cardholder data.
- Use firewalls and log configurations to safeguard data.
- Authorize only necessary inbound and outbound traffic.
3 Do Not Use Vendor-Supplied Defaults - Change default passwords and settings before system installation.
- Develop configuration standards for all system components.
4 Protect Cardholder Data - Store cardholder data only as needed and securely delete when no longer required.
- Mask the Primary Account Number (PAN) to limit visibility.
- Use strong cryptography for data protection.
5 Encrypt Transmission of Cardholder Data Across Open, Public Networks - Employ strong cryptographic technologies for data transmission.
- Ensure strong encryption is used in wireless environments transmitting cardholder data.
6 Maintain a Vulnerability Management Program - Deploy and regularly update anti-virus software on all vulnerable systems.
- Verify that anti-virus programs are working and logging correctly.
7 Develop and Maintain Secure Systems and Applications - Apply security patches within one month for all components and software.
- Implement a mechanism to identify and rank newly discovered vulnerabilities.
8 Restrict Access to Cardholder Data - Limit access to cardholder data to authorized personnel and programs only.
- Assign unique IDs to individuals with computer access.
9 Identify and Authenticate Access to Systems - Restrict physical access to cardholder data.
10 Track and Monitor Access to Network Resources and Cardholder Data - Implement logging mechanisms to track access.
- Regularly test security systems and processes.
11 Develop and Maintain a Comprehensive Policy - Create and maintain an information security policy addressing all PCI DSS requirements.
- Review the policy annually and distribute it to relevant personnel.

Additional Steps for Ongoing Compliance

Step Description Additional Steps & Details
1 Conduct Regular Training Ensure all employees are trained on PCI DSS requirements and security best practices.
2 Perform Regular Risk Assessments Identify and mitigate risks to cardholder data consistently through regular risk assessments.
3 Engage Qualified Security Assessors Conduct annual assessments with qualified security assessors to validate compliance status.
4 Complete Relevant SAQ or ROC Depending on transaction processing volume, complete the appropriate Self-Assessment Questionnaire (SAQ) or Report of Compliance (ROC).
