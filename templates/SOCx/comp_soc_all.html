Overview of SOC Reports

SOC 1, SOC 2, and SOC 3 reports are types of System and Organization Controls (SOC) reports designed to assess and communicate different aspects of service organizations' controls. These reports serve various purposes and audiences, providing insights into how organizations manage and protect data and systems.

What’s the difference between SOC 1, SOC 2, and SOC 3?

The main differences among SOC 1, SOC 2, and SOC 3 reports are in their focus and intended audience. Here’s a breakdown:

  • SOC 1 Report: Focuses on internal controls related to financial reporting. It is used by organizations whose services impact their clients' financial statements, ensuring that these controls are designed and operating effectively to support accurate financial reporting.
  • SOC 2 Report: Assesses controls related to the Trust Services Criteria: security, availability, processing integrity, confidentiality, and privacy. SOC 2 reports are intended for a more specialized audience, such as clients and business partners, who require detailed assurance on these criteria.
  • SOC 3 Report: Provides a summary of the SOC 2 report and is designed for public distribution. It offers a high-level overview of the service organization's controls related to security, availability, and confidentiality, making it suitable for a broader audience.
SOC 1 reports focus on financial reporting controls, SOC 2 reports assess controls based on Trust Services Criteria, and SOC 3 reports offer a public summary of SOC 2 controls.

Which SOC Report is Right for You?

  • SOC 1 Report: Ideal if your services impact your clients' financial operations. It provides assurance on financial reporting controls and is necessary for organizations handling financial transactions or processing payments.
  • SOC 2 Report: Best suited if you need to demonstrate detailed controls related to security, availability, processing integrity, confidentiality, and privacy. It is important for organizations with sensitive data or complex systems requiring comprehensive assurance.
  • SOC 3 Report: Appropriate for organizations aiming to provide a high-level summary of their controls to a broad audience. It is often used for public assurance and marketing purposes.
Your choice of SOC report should align with your organization’s needs and objectives, as well as the requirements of your clients and stakeholders. Evaluate the specific assurances needed to determine the most suitable SOC report for your organization.
