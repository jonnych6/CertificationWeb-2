How to reach SOC 1 Type 2 compliance

For many organizations, achieving SOC 1 Type 2 compliance is a critical milestone toward ensuring the reliability and integrity of their financial reporting over a specified period. To comply with SOC 1 Type 2, you need to establish and document robust internal controls over financial reporting, demonstrating that these controls are not only suitably designed but also operating effectively over time. We've developed a detailed checklist to guide you through your SOC 1 Type 2 implementation and compliance process. This checklist includes the following steps:

Step Description Additional Steps & Details
1 Scope Definition Extend the scope from Type 1 to include 24/7 monitoring of controls.
2 Organization of Project Team Same as Type 1 with the addition of resources for around-the-clock monitoring and assessment.
3 Documentation and Risk Assessment Update documentation and perform risk assessments continuously throughout the audit period.
4 Develop and Implement Controls Design and ensure controls are effectively operating over the audit period.
5 Continuous Evidence Collection Collect and organize evidence of control effectiveness throughout the audit period.
6 Regular Internal Review Perform periodic internal assessments to test and adjust controls as needed.
7 Ongoing Communication with the Auditor Maintain consistent communication with the auditor during interim testing.
8 Resolving Interim Findings Act on interim testing results to resolve issues before the audit period ends.
9 Completion of Audit and Report Collaborate with the auditor to finalize the review of control effectiveness.
Review and issue the final Type 2 report, ensuring it accurately represents control operations.
10 Post-Audit Activities Implement recommended modifications to improve control effectiveness.
Prepare for long-term compliance and the next audit.
