How to reach SOC 2 Type 1 compliance

For many organizations, achieving SOC 2 Type 1 compliance is a critical milestone toward ensuring the security, availability, processing integrity, confidentiality, and privacy of their systems and data. To comply with SOC 2 Type 1, you need to establish and document robust controls that demonstrate your commitment to these Trust Service Criteria at a specific point in time. We've developed a detailed checklist to guide you through your SOC 2 Type 1 implementation and compliance process. This checklist includes the following steps:

Step Description Additional Steps & Details
1 Planning Stage Define systems and processes to cover and choose relevant Trust Service Criteria (Security, Availability, Processing Integrity, Confidentiality, Privacy).
Form a project team with a project manager and key members from IT, security, compliance, and operations.
2 Preparation and Planning Conduct a risk assessment to identify existing controls, gaps, and high-risk areas.
Document and communicate relevant policies and procedures; implement necessary security controls and ensure data backup, disaster recovery, and business continuity plans.
3 Employee Training and Awareness Conduct security awareness training sessions and ensure all employees understand SOC 2 compliance requirements.
4 Gather Evidence Collect evidence of implemented controls and prepare necessary documents for the auditor (e.g., system descriptions, network diagrams, change management records).
5 Internal Control Testing Perform self-testing and self-evaluation of controls; adjust controls based on internal audit results.
6 Select an Auditor Research and select an experienced SOC 2 auditor based on expertise, cost, and fit.
7 The Audit Meet with the auditor to define scope, timeline, and expectations; provide necessary documentation and access during the audit.
8 Preliminary Findings Review Review initial findings with the auditor, address any issues, and develop a remediation plan.
9 Final Report Obtain and review the final report.
Share it with stakeholders and discuss findings.
10 Post-Audit Actions Remediate any findings and recommendations; continuously review and improve controls.
