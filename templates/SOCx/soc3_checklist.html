SOC 3

How to reach SOC 3 compliance

For organizations seeking to achieve SOC 3 compliance, this checklist provides a detailed guide through the SOC 3 implementation and compliance process. It includes steps to help ensure that your organization meets the necessary Trust Service Criteria.

>
Step Description Additional Steps & Details
1 Scope Definition
  • Identify systems and processes under the SOC 3 audit scope.
  • Determine applicability in the criteria (Security, Availability, Processing Integrity, Confidentiality, Privacy).
  • Conduct a comprehensive inventory of all IT systems, applications, and processes that store, process, or transmit sensitive data.
  • Develop detailed data flow diagrams to visualize how data moves through the organization and highlight critical control points.
2 Organization of Project Team
  • Designate a project manager who will oversee the SOC 3 compliance project.
  • Build a cross-functional team of IT, security, compliance, and operational resources.
  • Ensure team members are clear on their roles and responsibilities and maintain regular communication to facilitate collaboration and progress tracking.
3 Risk Assessment
  • Perform a risk identification and assessment of the scoped environment to find out and measure the risks.
  • Address gaps detected from previous audits or assessments and prioritize remediation efforts based on risk levels.
4 Develop and Refine Policies and Procedures
  • Revise policies and procedures to align with Trust Service Criteria.
  • Ensure all documents are clear, accessible, and understood by relevant staff.
  • Document processes to ensure every process is well-understood and documented.
5 Implement and Optimize Controls
  • Implement necessary controls to mitigate identified risks.
  • Ensure controls are aligned with industry best practices and regulatory requirements.
  • Implement continuous monitoring and logging to ensure control effectiveness.
  • Regularly test and update controls as needed.
6 Employee Training
  • Develop a comprehensive training program focused on SOC 3 compliance and security best practices.
  • Organize regular training sessions, workshops, and seminars.
  • Update training materials and sessions with changes in policies and procedures.
  • Include practical examples and case studies.
  • Conduct periodic assessments and quizzes to gauge employees' understanding and retention of the training material.
  • Collect feedback to continuously improve the training program.
7 Evidence Gathering
  • Continuously collect evidence demonstrating the effectiveness of implemented controls.
  • Types of evidence may include system logs, access control records, incident reports, change management records, and periodic audit reports.
  • Store evidence in a centralized and secure repository accessible to authorized personnel.
  • Prepare a summary report or dashboard highlighting key control activities and findings.
8 Periodic Internal Audits
  • Schedule regular internal audits to assess control effectiveness.
  • Develop a comprehensive internal audit plan outlining the scope, objectives, and schedule.
  • Document all findings, including deficiencies, weaknesses, or areas for improvement.
  • Analyze root causes and develop remediation plans.
  • Implement corrective actions and verify that changes effectively mitigate risks.
9 Hire an Auditor
  • Select an auditor experienced in SOC 3 audits.
  • Start discussions and agree on timelines covering the required review period (typically 6-12 months).
  • Ensure clear understanding and alignment on the audit scope, objectives, and deliverables.
10 Interacting with the Auditor
  • Maintain regular communication with the auditor throughout the audit process.
  • Allow interim reviews to ensure understanding and alignment.
  • Provide the auditor with necessary documentation, access, and support.
11 Act on Feedback
  • Discuss interim feedback or findings from the auditor and take necessary actions.
  • Develop and implement action plans to address any tracked issues.
  • Verify the effectiveness of remediation efforts.
12 Final Audit and Report
  • Assist the auditor in completing the control audit for the chosen period.
  • Provide additional information promptly to finalize the assessment.
  • Review the final audit report thoroughly.
  • Distribute the final report to appropriate stakeholders and discuss responses and required actions.
  • Develop and implement action plans based on audit findings and recommendations.
13 Ongoing Compliance Program

  • Adopt continuous monitoring and periodic control review processes.
  • Conduct regular risk assessments and update controls based on new threats.
  • Maintain thorough documentation of all compliance activities.
  • Continue investing in employee training and awareness programs.
