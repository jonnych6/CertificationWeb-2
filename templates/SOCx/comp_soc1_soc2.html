Overview of SOC Reports

The American Institute of Certified Public Accountants (AICPA) developed Service Organization Controls (SOC) reports to ensure businesses can confidently engage new vendors by verifying their data security and financial reporting practices. These reports are crucial for service organizations aiming to demonstrate their trustworthiness to clients.

SOC 1 and SOC 2 reports are commonly requested by buyers in North America to assess the reliability and security of their service providers.

The difference between a SOC 1 and a SOC 2 report lies in their focus: SOC 1 centers on financial operations, while SOC 2 emphasizes information security.

  • SOC 1 Report: This report details the controls your organization has in place to ensure accurate financial reporting and operations. It is primarily used by organizations whose services impact their clients' financial statements, providing assurance that their controls are designed and operating effectively for financial reporting.
  • SOC 2 Report: This report addresses data security and is based on five Trust Service Criteria: security, availability, processing integrity, confidentiality, and privacy. While every SOC 2 report evaluates security, the inclusion of the other four criteria depends on their relevance to your business. SOC 2 is essential for demonstrating that your information security practices are robust and can protect your customers' data.

Do you need a SOC1 or SOC2 report?

  • SOC 1 Report: If your services affect your clients' financial operations, a SOC 1 report is likely required. For instance, organizations that process payments or handle financial transactions on behalf of their clients will need a SOC 1 report to ensure accurate financial reporting.
  • SOC 2 Report: If you manage or interact with your customers' sensitive data, a SOC 2 report is necessary. This is especially common for cloud service providers, cybersecurity firms, and any organization responsible for safeguarding data. SOC 2 reports focus on information security and the Trust Service Criteria: security, availability, processing integrity, confidentiality, and privacy.
Your clients will determine the type of SOC report they require. However, it is recommended not to wait for these requests. Plan ahead of time for a SOC audit to minimize time and effort. Consider your products and services to anticipate the type of SOC report required by your customers, ensuring timely and efficient delivery.
