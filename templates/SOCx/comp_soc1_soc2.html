SOC 1 Type 1

How to reach SOC 1 Type 1 compliance

For many organizations, achieving SOC 1 Type 1 compliance is a critical milestone toward ensuring the reliability and integrity of their financial reporting. To comply with SOC 1 Type 1, you need to establish and document robust internal controls over financial reporting, demonstrating that these controls are suitably designed to meet the specified objectives at a specific point in time. We've developed a detailed checklist to guide you through your SOC 1 Type 1 implementation and compliance process. This checklist includes the following steps:

Step Description Additional Steps & Details
1 Scope Definition Identify and document systems and processes relevant to the selected Trust Service Criteria (Security, Availability, Processing Integrity, Confidentiality, Privacy).
Identify classes of transactions, systems involved, and applicable applications.
2 Organization of Project Team Appoint a project manager.
Assemble a team with members from IT, security, compliance, and operations.
3 Documentation and Risk Assessment Document all relevant processes, systems, and controls.
Conduct a risk assessment to identify potential risks to compliance.
4 Develop and Implement Controls Design and implement controls to address identified risks.
Ensure controls meet the criteria for security, availability, processing integrity, confidentiality, and privacy.
5 Document Preparation Gather supporting documentation.
Prepare clear and accessible documentation for the auditor.
6 Select an Auditor Select a reputable auditing firm or CPA experienced in SOC 2 reports.
Agree on the scope and timing of the audit.
7 The Period of Engagement Ensure the auditor understands the control environment.
Provide access to relevant documents and personnel.
8 Results Check and Adjust Review preliminary results with the auditor.
Resolve any discrepancies or misunderstandings before the final report.
9 Sharing Final Report Review the final Type 1 report for accuracy and completeness.
Share the report with stakeholders who rely on the proper implementation of controls for security and compliance.
