SOC 2 Type 2

How to reach SOC 2 Type 2 compliance

For many organizations, achieving SOC 2 Type 2 compliance is a critical milestone towards ensuring the reliability and integrity of their information security. To comply with SOC 2 Type 2, you need to establish and document robust internal controls over data protection, demonstrating that these controls are suitably designed to meet the specified objectives over a period of time. We've developed a detailed checklist to guide you through your SOC 2 Type 2 implementation and compliance process. This checklist includes the following steps:

Step Description Additional Steps & Details
1 Preliminary Steps - Identify systems and processes under the SOC 2 audit scope.
- Determine applicability in the criteria (Security, Availability, Processing Integrity, Confidentiality, Privacy).
- Designate a project manager and build a cross-functional team of IT, security, compliance, and operational resources.
2 Planning and Preparation - Perform a risk identification and assessment of the scoped environment.
- Address gaps detected from the SOC 2 Type 1 audit previously.
- Develop and refine policies and procedures.
- Implement and optimize necessary controls.
3 Training and Awareness - Ensure extensive training on safety practices and SOC 2 compliance requirements.
- Update training materials and sessions with changes in the policies and procedures.
4 Evidence Gathering - Gather evidence regarding the operational effectiveness of controls over the audit period.
- Ensure evidence is readily available and in an easily reviewable form for the auditor.
5 Control Testing - Periodically perform internal audits to determine operational effectiveness of controls.
- Use the findings to make necessary adjustments before the external audit begins.
6 Choose an Auditor - Select an auditor with the right experience with SOC 2 Type 2 audits.
- Agree on timelines with the auditor covering the required review period (6-12 months).
7 Auditing - Keep communicating with your auditor on a weekly basis.
- Allow interim reviews if necessary to ensure understanding and alignment of the control environments.
8 Interim Findings Review - Discuss and take action on any interim feedback or findings from the auditor.
- Outline action plans for any issues that are being tracked.
9 Final Audit and Report - Help the auditor complete control audit for the period chosen.
- Furnish promptly the auditor with any other information needed to finalize the assessment of controls.
- Perform a thorough review of final reports from the audit and distribute to appropriate stakeholders.
10 Ongoing Compliance Program - Implement the decisions taken by the auditor on the suggestions placed in the audit report.
- Adopt the process of ongoing control monitoring and periodic control review.
