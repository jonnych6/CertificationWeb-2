SOC 2 Type 2

How to reach SOC 2 Type 2 compliance

For many organizations, achieving SOC 2 Type 2 compliance is a critical milestone towards ensuring the reliability and integrity of their information security. To comply with SOC 2 Type 2, you need to establish and document robust internal controls over data protection, demonstrating that these controls are suitably designed to meet the specified objectives over a period of time. We've developed a detailed checklist to guide you through your SOC 2 Type 2 implementation and compliance process. This checklist includes the following steps:

Step Description Additional Steps & Details
1 Preliminary Steps
  • Definition of Scope:
    • Identify systems and processes under the SOC 2 audit scope.
    • Determine applicability in the criteria (Security, Availability, Processing Integrity, Confidentiality, Privacy).
    • Conduct a comprehensive inventory of all IT systems, applications, and processes that store, process, or transmit sensitive data.
    • Develop detailed data flow diagrams to visualize how data moves through the organization and highlight critical control points.
  • Constitute Project Team:
    • Designate a project manager who will oversee the SOC 2 compliance project.
    • Build a cross-functional team of IT, security, compliance, and operational resources.
    • Ensure team members are clear on their roles and responsibilities and maintain regular communication to facilitate collaboration and progress tracking.
2 Planning and Preparation
  • Risk Assessment:
    • Perform a risk identification and assessment of the scoped environment to find out and measure the risks.
    • Address gaps detected from the SOC 2 Type 1 audit previously.
    • Conduct a thorough gap analysis by comparing existing controls against the SOC 2 Trust Service Criteria requirements and document these gaps to prioritize remediation efforts.
    • Assess the impact and likelihood of potential risks to identify high-risk areas, and prioritize these areas based on the criticality of the systems and data involved, and the potential impact on the organization if a security incident were to occur.
  • Develop and Refine Policies and Procedures:
    • Revise and refine policies and procedures to align with the Trust Service Criteria.
    • Ensure documentation is clear and accessible to all relevant staff members.
    • Review all existing policies and procedures to ensure they align with the SOC 2 Trust Service Criteria.
    • Update or refine policies and procedures as necessary to address any changes in the scoped environment or new requirements identified during the risk assessment.
    • Ensure that policies and procedures are comprehensive, covering all relevant aspects of security, availability, processing integrity, confidentiality, and privacy.
  • Implement and Optimize Controls:
    • Establish or strengthen necessary controls to provide sufficient countermeasures for identified risks.
    • Implement continuous monitoring and logging to ensure control effectiveness.
    • Implement new controls or enhance existing ones to mitigate identified risks effectively.
    • Ensure that controls are aligned with industry best practices and regulatory requirements.
    • Develop and implement a robust data backup strategy that includes regular backups, secure storage, and routine testing of backup procedures.
    • Create a comprehensive disaster recovery plan that outlines the steps to restore systems and data in the event of a catastrophic incident.
    • Develop a business continuity plan to ensure that critical business functions can continue during and after a disaster, and regularly test and update both the disaster recovery and business continuity plans to ensure their effectiveness.
3 Training and Awareness
  • Employee Training:
    • Ensure extensive training on safety practices and SOC 2 compliance requirements.
    • Update training materials and sessions with changes in policies and procedures.
    • Develop a comprehensive training program focused on SOC 2 compliance and security best practices.
    • Organize regular training sessions, workshops, and seminars to educate employees on security best practices and their role in maintaining compliance.
    • Include practical examples and case studies to help employees understand the real-world implications of SOC 2 compliance and security practices.
    • Provide detailed training on the Trust Service Criteria (Security, Availability, Processing Integrity, Confidentiality, and Privacy) and how they relate to the organization’s operations.
    • Explain the specific controls and policies in place to meet SOC 2 requirements and how employees can contribute to their effectiveness.
    • Train staff on recognizing and reporting security incidents, phishing attempts, and other potential threats.
    • Ensure that employees understand the importance of following security protocols, such as password management, access controls, and data handling procedures.
    • Clearly communicate the roles and responsibilities of each employee in maintaining SOC 2 compliance and provide easy access to relevant policies, procedures, and guidelines that employees need to follow.
    • Regularly reinforce key messages through reminders, newsletters, and ongoing communications, and conduct periodic assessments and quizzes to gauge employees' understanding and retention of the training material.
    • Collect feedback from employees on the training sessions to continuously improve the training program and address any gaps in knowledge.
4 Evidence Gathering
  • Ongoing Documentation:
    • Gather evidence of the operational effectiveness of controls over the audit period and document it.
    • Ensure evidence is readily available and in an easily reviewable form for the auditor.
    • Continuously collect evidence that demonstrates the effectiveness of the implemented controls throughout the audit period.
    • Types of evidence may include system logs, access control records, incident reports, change management records, and periodic audit reports.
    • Ensure evidence is collected in real-time or as close to real-time as possible to provide an accurate and comprehensive record of control operations.
    • Organize and store evidence in a centralized and secure repository that is easily accessible to authorized personnel.
    • Maintain detailed records of all compliance activities, including monitoring efforts, internal audits, training sessions, and risk assessments.
    • Prepare a summary report or dashboard that provides an overview of the collected evidence and highlights key control activities and findings.
    • Schedule regular meetings with the auditor to review the evidence and address any questions or concerns.
    • Be proactive in providing additional context or explanations for the evidence to facilitate the auditor's understanding and assessment.
5 Control Testing
  • Periodic Internal Audits:
    • Periodically perform internal audits to determine the operational effectiveness of controls.
    • Use findings from internal audits to make necessary adjustments before the external audit begins.
    • Develop and document action plans to address any identified issues or recommendations.
    • Schedule regular internal audits throughout the audit period to assess the effectiveness of controls.
    • Develop a comprehensive internal audit plan that outlines the scope, objectives, and schedule for the audits.
    • Use a risk-based approach to prioritize areas for internal audit, focusing on high-risk controls and processes.
    • Document all findings from the internal audits, including any identified deficiencies, weaknesses, or areas for improvement.
    • Analyze the root causes of any issues found during the internal audits to develop effective remediation plans.
    • Assign responsibility for addressing each finding to specific team members or departments, ensuring accountability and timely resolution.
    • Implement corrective actions to address identified issues, and verify that the changes effectively mitigate the risks.
    • Perform follow-up audits to confirm that the corrective actions have been successfully implemented and that the controls are now operating effectively.
6 Choose an Auditor
  • Hire an Auditor:
    • Select and start discussions with an auditor experienced in SOC 2 Type 2 audits if not done in Type 1.
    • Agree on timelines with the third-party auditor covering the required review period (normally 6-12 months).
    • Ensure clear understanding and alignment on the audit scope, objectives, and deliverables.
7 Auditing
  • Interacting with the Auditor:
    • Maintain weekly communication with the auditor from the opening meeting to the final audit.
    • Allow interim reviews to ensure understanding and alignment of the control environments.
    • Provide the auditor with all necessary documentation, access, and support throughout the audit process.
8 Interim Findings Review
  • Act on Feedback:
    • Discuss and take action on any interim feedback or findings from the auditor related to the operational effectiveness of controls.
    • Develop and implement action plans to address any tracked issues before the end of the audit period.
    • Maintain open communication with the auditor and verify the effectiveness of remediation efforts.
9 Final Audit and Report
  • Actual Execution of Audit:
    • Assist the auditor in completing the control audit for the chosen period.
    • Provide any additional information promptly to finalize the assessment of controls.
  • Finalize Report and Distribute:
    • Perform a thorough review of the final audit reports.
    • Distribute the final report to appropriate stakeholders and discuss resultant responses and required actions.
    • Develop and implement action plans based on audit findings and recommendations.
10 Ongoing Compliance Program
  • Adopt Auditor's Suggestions:
    • Implement the decisions taken by the auditor on the suggestions placed in the audit report.
    • Document and verify all changes made in response to the auditor’s suggestions.
  • Prepare for Future Compliance:
    • Adopt ongoing control monitoring and periodic control review processes.
    • Conduct regular risk assessments and update controls based on new threats and vulnerabilities.
    • Maintain thorough documentation of all compliance activities for future audits.
    • Continue to invest in employee training and awareness programs.
